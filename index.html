Why not to hate C++

Tue, Jun 6 2017

I've been working seriously with C++ for about one year and I would like to share my experience with one of the most important programming languages of all time, also one of the most hated.

C++ is definitely hard to learn, to program and to compile. GCC sucks. Seriously, don't even try to use it. Prefer clang or something else.

C++ is mind-blown. As you code in C++, your brain will suffer from eternal neural injuries. You'll always be worried about multi-inheritance and dangling pointers. You'll try hard to put a lambda expression and face days of headache trying to understand why somebody could create such a weird syntax.

However, C++ is really powerful. You can possibly do anything in C++. In case you didn't know, Nietzsche was coded in C++ even having no C++ at the time. See, you can't just go code something in the past with Java or Python.

Imagine you have a family of rabbits. As a expert programmer with years of experience in object-oriented programming, you'll define a class Rabbit, and maybe a factory of rabbits if you're also experienced with design patterns. But think about it a second: why not inhering Rabbit from a class like Apple and Walrus, at the same time? Well, you can just do this. You can do whatever you want to.

But you can also forget about all this crap of OO and migrate to COBOL paradigm. Putting some goto's in the code won't hurt you, you're not a coward. It will work, that's it. That is C++.

But that kind of thing you can do with any language - except for Haskell, you can't do nothing but struggle with Haskell. Well, let's try to cast a class to any class with horrible_cast (Google is your friend). You can. Let's try to fuck up with you RAM and allocate a giant elephant of dangling pointers addressing kernel variables and modify everything expecting your PC to burn in hell along with the last code written in Pascal: you can. You just can. I just don't know why but you can, try it one of these days.

And I haven't mentioned webservers in C++. With SSL. It's insane. Unit testing? Mocking framework? Setting private to public with a goddamn compiler macro? Yes, you can.

That kind of power should not be hated, it must be appreciated. One must take advantage of C++ power and use for the good, possibly taking down advanced pseudocodes like Python and Perl. Well, the latter is anything but a pseudocode. And I love Python. But I also love C++, so share this love with me if you can and start writing your own Makefile today.

C-x C-s.
